When a loan request is received, approved, rejected, disbursed, or early settled, a notification is sent to the member.
A member is eligible for a loan after one full year from the date of his membership, and his fund account balance shall not be less than 6000 SAR.
A member may borrow up to double his fund account balance.
When wishing to borrow, the member completes a loan application form and submits along with the request.
The borrowing member is exempt from paying the monthly contribution installment; repayment of loan installments to the Fund’s account begins in the month following receipt of the loan disbursement. For example, if the loan is disbursed before the due date of the July 5th for the contribution cycle of June and he has not made the contribution; he is except from the contribution of June and Repayment starts for the Contribution cycle of July. Another example, if the loan is disbursed after the due date of the July 5th for the contribution cycle of July is exempted and repayments starts for the cycle of August.
A member may borrow up to double the balance of his fund account. When disbursing the loan from the fund's master account, it is thought of in two parts: member's portion and master fund portion. The Disbursement is funded from the member's fund account until it is exhausted then from the master fund account for what remains.
Disbursing a loan will debit the master fund account and the member's fund account equally.
The borrowing member completes his loan after paying all that is due to the Master Fund and when his new fund account balance reaches 16% of the total amount he borrowed.
The loan is considered paid off when the master fund account portion is paid off and topped of with another 16% of the original loan amount. The percentage needs to be configurable via a setting page.
The borrowing member must complete the loan application form; the guarantor must be only from among other members with income, plus two witnesses. The guarantor member is released from his guarantee when the borrower has fully settled what is due to the Master Fund.
The parent member of a borrowing dependent provide the loan repayment installments similar to the contribution allocation to dependents.
If the guarantor member withdraws or his membership is cancelled, his guarantee continues for the borrowing member until the borrowing member settles the Fund’s dues, or the borrowing member is required to name another guarantor member.
If the borrowing member defaults on repayment for two repayment cycles (consecutive or not), he is notified that his membership will be cancelled, and the installment may be debited from his guarantor if he defaults a third cycle; the guarantor is notified accordingly.
If the borrowing member defaults three cycles (consecutive or not), the guarantor is notified, then the amount is debited from his fund account (the guarantor’s fund account); each subsequent installment the borrower fails to pay on that same loan is handled the same way.
