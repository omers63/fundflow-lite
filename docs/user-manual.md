## 1. Welcome to FundFlow

FundFlow is a member-owned cooperative fund designed to help members save and borrow money through a trusted, structured system. As a member you benefit from:

- **Monthly contributions** that grow your personal fund stake.
- **Access to loans** from the collective fund at no interest.
- **A transparent ledger** — you can see every transaction on your accounts at any time.
- **Family support** — parent members can sponsor dependents and fund their accounts.

## 2. Getting Started

### 2.1 Applying for Membership

If you are not yet a member, submit a membership application through the FundFlow application form. Provide your:
- Full name
- Email address
- Phone number
- City of residence
- Purpose / reason for joining

Your application will be reviewed by the fund's administrators. You will receive an **email notification** once a decision is made.

> If approved, you will receive your member number and login credentials to access the portal.

**URL:** `/member`

Log in with the email and password provided upon approval. If you have forgotten your password, use the **Forgot Password** link on the login page.

## 4. Contributions

### 4.1 How Contributions Work

Every active member contributes a fixed amount each month to the collective fund. This contribution:

1. **Debits your cash account** (the money you have deposited into your account).
2. **Credits the master fund** (the collective pool).
3. **Credits your personal fund account** (building your ownership stake in the fund).

Your **fund account balance** is important — it determines:
- How much you can borrow (up to 2× your fund balance).
- Whether you meet the minimum fund balance requirement for loan eligibility.

**Contribution cycle timeline:**

| Date | What Happens |
|------|--------------|
| 1st of each month | You receive a **notification** reminding you to ensure funds are in your cash account |
| 5th of each month | Your contribution is **automatically deducted** from your cash account |
| After the 5th | Contribution is applied but marked as **late** |

---

### 4.4 How My Contribution is Applied

On the **5th of each month**, the system automatically:

1. Checks your cash account balance.
2. If sufficient: deducts the contribution amount and credits the fund accounts.
3. Sends you an **account statement notification** confirming the transaction.

If your cash account **does not have enough funds** on the 5th, your contribution will not be applied automatically. Contact the fund administrator or ensure funds are available before the deadline.

---

### 4.5 Funding Your Cash Account

Your cash account is the source from which both contributions and loan repayments are deducted. To add funds:

1. Make a bank transfer to the fund's bank account.
2. Contact the fund administrator to have the transfer **posted** to your cash account.
3. Alternatively, if your parent member has agreed to fund your account, they can transfer funds from their own cash account to yours (see §8.3).

> Once posted, the funds appear in your cash account immediately.

---

### 4.6 Contribution Exemption During a Loan

If you have an **active loan**, you are **automatically exempt** from monthly contributions for the entire loan duration. Your loan repayments take the place of contributions.

Contributions resume automatically once your loan is fully settled.

## 5. Loans

### 5.1 Loan Eligibility

Before applying for a loan, you must meet **all** of the following conditions:

| Condition | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Membership status | Must be **active** |
| Membership duration | At least **12 months** from your joining date |
| Fund account balance | At least **SAR 6,000** |
| No overdue installments | All prior loan installments must be paid |

If you do not yet meet these requirements, the loan application form will display the specific reason and your eligibility date.

---

### 5.2 How Much Can I Borrow?

**Maximum loan amount = 2 × your fund account balance**

For example:
- If your fund account balance is **SAR 20,000**, you may borrow up to **SAR 40,000**.
- If your fund account balance is **SAR 60,000**, you may borrow up to **SAR 120,000**.

The loan amount must also fall within one of the **Loan Tier** ranges:

| Tier | Loan Amount Range (SAR) | Minimum Monthly Installment (SAR) |
|------|------------------------|------------------------------------|
| 1 | 1,000 — 30,000 | 1,000 |
| 2 | 31,000 — 60,000 | 1,500 |
| 3 | 61,000 — 90,000 | 2,000 |
| 4 | 91,000 — 120,000 | 2,500 |
| 5 | 121,000 — 150,000 | 3,000 |
| 6 | 151,000 — 180,000 | 3,500 |
| 7 | 181,000 — 210,000 | 4,000 |
| 8 | 211,000 — 240,000 | 4,500 |
| 9 | 241,000 — 270,000 | 5,000 |
| 10 | 271,000 — 300,000 | 5,500 |

Your monthly repayment must be **at least** the minimum for your tier.

### 5.3 Applying for a Loan

**Navigation:** My Finance → My Loans → Apply for Loan

Click **Apply for Loan** to open the application form.

**Required information:**

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Loan Amount (SAR)** | The amount you wish to borrow (within your eligible maximum) |
| **Repayment Period** | Number of months (1–36) over which you will repay |
| **Purpose** | A clear description of why you need the loan |
| **Guarantor Member** | An active fund member who agrees to guarantee your loan |
| **Witness 1 — Name** | Full name of the first witness |
| **Witness 1 — Phone** | Phone number of the first witness (optional) |
| **Witness 2 — Name** | Full name of the second witness |
| **Witness 2 — Phone** | Phone number of the second witness (optional) |

> **Guarantor requirements:** The guarantor must be an **active fund member**. They are responsible for your repayments if you default beyond the grace period.

After submitting, you receive a **LoanSubmittedNotification** confirming your application is under review.

---

### 5.4 After Submission: Loan Lifecycle

Your loan moves through the following stages:

| Status | Meaning | What Happens |
|--------|---------|--------------|
| **Pending** | Application received | Administrator reviews your application |
| **Approved** | Application approved | Loan amount, installments, and fund tier confirmed; you are placed in the queue |
| **Active** | Loan disbursed | Funds credited to your account; installment schedule begins |
| **Completed** | Loan fully settled | Automatic settlement after all conditions met |
| **Early Settled** | Early payoff processed | All remaining installments cleared at once |
| **Rejected** | Application declined | Reason provided; you may apply again once eligible |
| **Cancelled** | Application withdrawn | Cancelled before disbursement (by you or the administrator) |

You receive a **notification** at every status change.

---

### 5.5 Cancelling a Pending Application

**Navigation:** My Finance → My Loans

If your loan is still **Pending**, you may cancel it:

1. Find the loan in the table.
2. Click **Cancel** (the X icon in the Actions column).
3. Confirm the cancellation.

> Once a loan is **Approved** or **Active**, cancellation is only possible through the fund administrator.

---

### 5.6 Loan Disbursement

After approval, the administrator disburses the loan. Here is how the disbursement works:

**The loan is funded in two parts:**

| Part | Source | Amount |
|------|--------|--------|
| **Your portion** | Your fund account | Up to your current fund balance |
| **Fund's portion** | Master fund account | The remainder |

**Example:** You borrow **SAR 50,000** and your fund balance is **SAR 20,000**.
- Your portion: **SAR 20,000** (debited from your fund account → it drops to SAR 0).
- Fund's portion: **SAR 30,000** (debited from the master fund).

This split matters for settlement: your guarantor is released once the **fund's SAR 30,000 portion** is repaid.

**After disbursement you receive:**
- A `LoanDisbursedNotification` showing the disbursement split, first repayment month, and exempted contribution month.

---

### 5.7 Loan Repayments

Repayments follow the same monthly schedule as contributions:

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| **1st of Month M+1** | You receive a repayment reminder notification |
| **5th of Month M+1** | Repayment automatically deducted from your cash account |
| After the 5th | Repayment applied, flagged as **late** |

**Each repayment:**
- Debits your **cash account** (the full installment amount).
- Credits the **master fund** (returning the fund's capital).
- Credits your **personal fund account** (rebuilding your stake).

Your fund balance grows with each repayment. This is important — the loan is not considered settled until your fund balance reaches **16% of the original loan amount** (see §5.8).

**Ensuring your cash account is funded before the 5th is your responsibility.** If the system cannot debit your account on the 5th, the installment becomes overdue.

---

### 5.8 Understanding the Settlement Condition

Your loan is considered **fully settled** when **both** of the following are true:

| Condition | What It Means |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Fund's portion repaid** | All installment credits have repaid the `master_portion` of your loan |
| **Fund balance threshold reached** | Your fund account balance ≥ 16% of your original loan amount |

**Example:** You borrowed **SAR 50,000**.
- Fund's portion (master): **SAR 30,000** — must be fully repaid through installments.
- Threshold: **16% × SAR 50,000 = SAR 8,000** — your fund account must hold at least SAR 8,000.

The 16% threshold is set by the fund administrator and may be adjusted (it is captured on your loan at the time of disbursement and does not change retroactively).

When both conditions are met, your loan is **automatically marked as completed** and you receive a settlement notification. You then resume monthly contributions from the next cycle.

---

### 5.9 Early Settlement

If you wish to pay off your loan before the scheduled end date, contact your fund administrator to request **Early Settlement**.

The administrator will:
1. Verify your cash account has sufficient balance for all remaining installments.
2. Apply all remaining installments at once.
3. Mark your loan as **Early Settled**.

You will receive a `LoanEarlySettledNotification` confirming the closure.

---

### 5.10 Guarantor Responsibilities

When you name a guarantor on your loan, that member accepts the following responsibilities:

| Situation | Guarantor's Role |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Normal repayments | None — the guarantor is not involved |
| 1st or 2nd missed repayment | You receive warnings; guarantor is not yet liable |
| 3rd+ missed repayment | The overdue installment is **debited from the guarantor's fund account** |

**The guarantor is released** from their obligation when the **fund's portion of the loan** (`master_portion`) has been fully repaid through your installments. The guarantor receives a notification when released.

**Important for guarantors:** Even if the guarantor's membership is cancelled or they withdraw from the fund, their obligation continues until the borrower fully repays the fund's portion.

> **Be considerate of your guarantor:** Ensure your cash account is funded before the 5th of each month to avoid placing a financial burden on the person who trusted you.

---

### 5.11 What Happens If I Miss Repayments?

Missed repayments are tracked cumulatively (consecutive or non-consecutive) on your loan.

| Number of Missed Repayments | Consequence |
|-----------------------------|-------------|
| **1st or 2nd** (within grace period) | You receive a **default warning** notifying you that your membership may be cancelled if defaults continue |
| **3rd or more** | The overdue installment is **debited from your guarantor's fund account**; your guarantor is notified |
| **Each subsequent missed repayment** on the same loan | Same: guarantor's fund debited and notified |

Your **membership may be cancelled** if defaults continue unresolved. Contact the fund administrator as soon as possible if you are unable to make a repayment.

## 8. My Dependents (Parent Members Only)

The **My Dependents** section is visible **only** if you have been designated as a parent (sponsor) of one or more other members.

As a parent, you take on financial responsibility for your dependents' contributions and, if applicable, their loan repayments.

### 8.1 Viewing Your Dependents

**Navigation:** My Finance → My Dependents

The table shows each dependent member with:

| Column | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Member # | Dependent's member number |
| Name | Dependent's full name |
| Monthly Allocation | The contribution amount set for this dependent |
| Status | Dependent's membership status |
| Cash Balance | Current cash available in the dependent's account |

---

### 8.2 Setting a Dependent's Contribution Allocation

You can set how much your dependent contributes each month.

1. Click **Set Allocation** on the dependent's row.
2. Select the new amount (SAR 500–3,000 in steps of 500).
3. Confirm.

The dependent can also change their own allocation from their own portal. The most recently saved value applies.

---

### 8.3 Funding a Dependent's Cash Account

To transfer funds from your cash account to a dependent's cash account:

1. Click **Fund Cash Account** on the dependent's row.
2. Enter the amount to transfer (SAR).
3. Add an optional note (e.g., "June contribution + repayment").
4. Confirm.

**What happens:**
- Your cash account is **debited** by the specified amount.
- The dependent's cash account is **credited** by the same amount.

> Your cash balance must be sufficient for the transfer. If not, an error message will be shown. Top up your own cash account first before funding your dependents.

This mechanism covers both **contribution funding** and **loan repayment funding** for dependents.

## 10. Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: When is my contribution due?**
A: Contributions for any given month are due by the **5th of the following month**. For example, June's contribution is due by 5 July. You will receive a reminder on 1 July.

---

**Q: What if I don't have enough cash in my account on the 5th?**
A: Your contribution (or repayment) will not be processed. If this happens with a contribution, it remains unpaid for that period — contact the administrator. If it's a loan repayment, the installment becomes overdue and is tracked as a default.

---

**Q: How do I add money to my cash account?**
A: Transfer funds to the fund's bank account, then notify the administrator with your member number and transfer reference. The administrator will post the transaction to your cash account.

---

**Q: Can I have more than one loan at a time?**
A: Yes. There is no restriction on the number of simultaneous active loans, provided you meet the eligibility criteria for each new application.

---

**Q: When am I exempt from contributions?**
A: You are exempt from monthly contributions while you have an **active or approved loan**. The exemption applies from the month of disbursement (or the prior open cycle if disbursed on or before the 5th). Contributions resume after your loan is settled.

---

**Q: How do I know when my guarantor obligation ends?**
A: If you are a guarantor, you are released when the borrower's **fund's portion** (`master_portion`) of the loan is fully repaid. You will receive a system notification when this happens.

---

**Q: My loan shows SAR 0 in the fund tier but I've already been approved — why?**
A: Your loan has been approved and placed in the **queue** for the assigned fund tier. The administrator will disburse it when funds are available within that tier's allocation. You will receive a `LoanDisbursedNotification` when this happens.

---

**Q: What is the 16% settlement threshold?**
A: To protect the fund, a loan is not considered fully settled until:
1. All fund-portion installments are repaid, **and**
2. Your fund account balance reaches **16% of the original loan amount**.

Since every repayment credits both the master fund and your fund account, your fund balance grows naturally over the repayment period. In most cases, settlement is automatic.

---

**Q: Can I change my guarantor after disbursement?**
A: Not through the portal. Contact your fund administrator if your guarantor needs to be changed. Note that the current guarantor remains liable until the fund's portion is repaid, even if a new guarantor is named.

---

**Q: I'm a parent — can I also be a dependent of someone else?**
A: No. The system enforces that a member with dependents cannot be assigned a parent. This prevents circular or multi-level chains that would complicate financial accountability.

---

**Q: How do I download my monthly statements?**
A: Go to **My Finance → My Statements**. Find the month and click **Download PDF**.
